About this class!

Generally with classes I take, like most students I suspect, we generally like to find out how the class is in terms of difficulty, courseload, how the professor is, etc. I generally try to ask fellow students on the school's facebook page (that is if I even have my FB account activated ,you can find out about that on my blog section). Then I always look on the website ratemyprofessor! When I saw the reviews for this professor, I was thinking this class would be horrible. Apparently these were the main problems with the class:
  • The lab's are difficult
  • The professor is of no help, leaves you on your own whole semester, never said a word other then the first day of class the whole semester
  • The lab reports are extremely long, don't expect an A unless 50 page reports
  • The professor is mean
But how wrong they were. I've generally learned this is how ratemyprofessors work. Students who've done bad go on there and give horrible ratings cause they are mad at the professor for not passing them for just showing up.
I remember the first day of class after the lab workers explained the dangers and what not of the tools and where to pick up our lab-kits, our professor began to talk. He eventually got to say I know I see on rate my professor 'Oh need 50 page lab report for A', not true! just give me good amount, 20-30 pages, and follow this format! He then proceeded to write the format for the lab-report on the board. I wrote this down and always followed it!
Long story short I did extremely well in this class, I got an A. Halfway through my group members dropped the class since they couldn't handle the courseload, so I was on my own. I was the only one scoring 90+'s on each lab. Go check out my blog post on it to find out how I made it through this class and how I did well!
